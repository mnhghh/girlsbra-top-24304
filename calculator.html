How to Measure Correctly

Band Measurement

Measure around your ribcage, just under your bust. Keep the tape measure level and snug but not tight. Exhale and take the measurement.

Bust Measurement

Measure around the fullest part of your bust. Keep the tape measure level and parallel to the floor. Don't pull too tight.

Understanding Bra Sizes

Band Size Guide

The band provides 80% of your bra's support. It should be snug but comfortable, sitting parallel to the floor.

  • 32: 28-29 inches under bust
  • 34: 30-31 inches under bust
  • 36: 32-33 inches under bust
  • 38: 34-35 inches under bust
  • 40: 36-37 inches under bust

Cup Size Guide

Cup size is determined by the difference between your bust and band measurements.

  • A: 1 inch difference
  • B: 2 inch difference
  • C: 3 inch difference
  • D: 4 inch difference
  • DD: 5 inch difference

Bra Fitting Tips

Signs of a Good Fit

  • Band sits level around your body
  • Cups contain all breast tissue
  • Straps don't dig into shoulders
  • Center gore lies flat against chest
  • No spillage or gaps

When to Get Remeasured

  • Weight change of 10+ pounds
  • Pregnancy or breastfeeding
  • Hormonal changes
  • Every 6-12 months
  • When bras feel uncomfortable
